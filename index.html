Frequently Asked Questions

About the Product
What exactly is a Yellow Croaker?

Yellow Croaker (大黄鱼, Dàhuángyú) is a prized fish native to the East China Sea, beloved in Chinese cuisine for its delicate white flesh and distinctive rich flavor. When fried whole at the right temperature, the bones become completely edible and crunchy — that's where Crunch Fin's signature texture comes from.

Are the bones safe to eat?

Yes! This is one of the most common questions we get. Our precision frying process renders the small bones completely crispy and safe to eat — they're actually the best part. The bones are what give Crunch Fin its unmistakable satisfying crunch. Larger spine bones may still require care, but our production process ensures consistent, snackable results.

Are Crunch Fin snacks gluten-free?

Our Original Sea Salt flavor is gluten-free. The Sichuan Mala, Salted Egg Yolk, and Truffle Pepper flavors contain soy sauce and may not be suitable for those with gluten intolerance. All products are produced in a facility that also processes wheat. Please check the Nutrition Info page for full allergen details.

How spicy is the Sichuan Mala flavor?

We'd rate it a solid 7 out of 10. The heat comes primarily from dried chili, but the Sichuan peppercorn adds a mouth-numbing (mala) sensation that's more complex than pure heat. If you enjoy Korean buldak or spicy ramen, you'll handle it well. If you're new to mala, have a drink nearby.

How long do the snacks stay fresh?

Each resealable pouch has a shelf life of 12 months from the production date (see the bottom of the bag). Once opened, we recommend consuming within 2–3 weeks for peak crunch. Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
